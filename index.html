Home Phone Deals

MAY 2020

Traditional home phones are a thing of the past. While millenials mostly use cellphones, our seniors still rely on home phones. VOIP is much cheaper than traditional telephone jacks and also offer more features and it relies on the internet. Tech Dot compiled a list of the best home phone services available.

1. Fongo Home Phone: 4.95$ per month

Pros: Canada-wide, 29$ for phone adapter
Cons: High long distance rates, 9.95$ for number transfer

2. Carrytel: 4.99$ per month

Pros: Canada-wide, Low long distance rates
Cons: Activation fee of 50$ (including number transfer), Carrytel Internet Required

3. FreePhoneLine: 99.95$ one-time fee

Pros: No monthly cost, Canada-Wide
Cons: 35$ per 911 call, No support, High long distance rates

4. Ooma: 6$ per month

Pros: Canada-wide, Low cost (~5 to 6$ depending on province)
Cons: High initial cost for device (100$), 39.95$ for number transfer, High long distance rates

5. MagicJack: 40$ USD per year

Pros: Low cost, Canada + USA wide
Cons: Reliability, 19.95$ USD for number transfer + additional 10$ each year, USD price, High long distance rates
